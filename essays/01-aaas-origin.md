# I Had to Invent a Word: The Origin of AaaS Before the Industry Caught Up

On April 1, 2025, I wrote a term into a project file that didn't exist yet.

**AaaS — AI as a Solution.**

Not AI as a Service. Not Agent as a Service. AI as the *delivered solution*. The completed outcome. The audit that's already done. The booking that's already confirmed. The dispatch that already happened while you were reading this sentence.

Seven weeks later, GooApps published the first major public article defining "Agent as a Service" — framing it as a SaaS replacement where you hand an agent a task. That's not what I built.

- **Industry AaaS** = "Here is an agent. Go give it a task."
- **Neuruh AaaS** = "Here is the completed result. The agent already did the work."

One gives you a tool. The other gives you an outcome.

I knew the distinction mattered because I'd already solved the two problems that made it possible: hallucination and memory.

**Context Anchoring Protocol (CAP)** solves hallucination. Every agent output is anchored to verifiable context. No context, no output. The agent doesn't guess.

**Prompt-as-Memory Loop Architecture (PMLA)** solves memory. Agents maintain persistent state across sessions, tasks, and tool calls.

CAP + PMLA = agents that remember what they're doing and don't make things up. Everything else — AXON Gateway, DISPATCH-IQ, Baker HVAC, ROOF-OPS, the entire Neuruh stack — is built on top of it.

## Timeline

- **April 1, 2025** — AaaS coined in Neuruh project files
- **May 19, 2025** — GooApps publishes first "Agent as a Service" article
- **December 2025** — Neuruh white paper published
- **April 2026** — 370+ IP entries, 7 live deployments, 2 active revenue clients

The industry caught the name. Neuruh built the machine.
